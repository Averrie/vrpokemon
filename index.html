Project idea

Pokemon is one of the best-selling video game series in history and playing it in virtual reality has always been one of my biggest childhood fantasies. So I decided to build a VR Pokemon experience for my final project.

I know it’s not as cool and exciting as the final project of peers but I do think this experiment can lead many possibilities. Because all of the Pokemon games are built for game console, if I can build a mock VR Pokemon, then many other games on console will be probably viable for virtual experiences. I’m sure in terms of virtual reality, video games would be a very relatable topic.

Video

How did I build it

I mainly used Unity 4 and Blender to build my project. I used Blender to import the models and materials made by the community and fix the texture so that they will be rendered correctly inside of Unity. I also used Blender to add armature to the models and create animations using the armatures. That's how I made the Snorlax breathe.

The most difficult part for building my project is to paint the terrain. Unity only provides circular brushes so I had to create a rectangular brush and paint the terrain pixel by pixel. I really like the how my project end up looking after I paint it scrutinously. Since I don’t have enough time, viewers can only explore in the scene I built and I did’t make any interactions for my project. I will try to improve on that in the future.

Here are what I think would be great to add into my project: battle scenes, interactions with the Snorlax, scenes inside of building and caves and introduction scene where Professor Oak talks about the background of Pokemon and let the viewer to choose three starter units. Using Pikachu by default is also a good idea.

Conclusion

I really like the result of my final project and I had a lot fun building it. When I first tried it myself, the cartoon style background make it a pretty comfortable VR experience for me. The shadows were sort of broken when I tried it in lab though, I guess it's because of the shadow distance setting in Unity's rendering. Nonetheless, my project is less creative or high-skill-needed than the ones of many other people but given the time and the fact that I made this VR experience myself, I’m pretty satisfied with the result
